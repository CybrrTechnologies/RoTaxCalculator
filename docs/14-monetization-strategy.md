## Receiving Payments as SRL

### Overview

To receive income legally as an SRL (Limited Liability Company) in Romania, you need to:
1. Issue invoices (facturi)
2. Declare income to ANAF
3. Pay corporate taxes
4. Optionally pay yourself dividends

### Payment Methods by Revenue Source

#### 1. Donations (Buy Me a Coffee, Ko-fi, PayPal)

**How it works**:
- Users donate via platform (PayPal, Stripe)
- Platform takes 3-5% fee
- Funds arrive in your PayPal/Stripe account
- Transfer to SRL bank account

**SRL Accounting**:
1. **Receive payment**: Transfer from PayPal ‚Üí SRL bank account
2. **Issue invoice**: Self-invoice or revenue receipt (chitan»õƒÉ)
3. **Declare**: Include in quarterly VAT declaration (if VAT registered)
4. **Tax**: Pay 1-3% corporate tax (micro-company regime)

**Key Question**: Are donations taxable income?
- **If positioned as "tip/donation"**: May be non-taxable (check with accountant)
- **If positioned as "payment for service"**: Taxable as service revenue
- **Recommendation**: Position as "voluntary support" but declare as revenue to be safe

**Documents Needed**:
- Monthly bank statement showing PayPal transfers
- PayPal/Stripe transaction reports
- Self-invoices or revenue receipts (chitan»õe)

---

#### 2. Affiliate Commissions (XTB, Trading212, etc.)

**How it works**:
- Broker sends monthly commission report
- Broker may issue invoice TO you or require you to invoice THEM
- Payment via bank transfer (usually EUR or RON)

**SRL Accounting**:

**Option A: Broker invoices you** (you receive invoice from them)
1. Receive invoice from broker (e.g., "XTB sends you invoice for ‚Ç¨500 commission")
2. Register as expense? NO - this is income to you
3. Actually: Broker should issue a **payment notice**, not invoice
4. You issue **invoice TO broker** for services rendered
5. Receive payment to SRL bank account
6. Declare as revenue

**Option B: You invoice broker** (most common)
1. Broker sends commission report (e.g., "You earned ‚Ç¨500 this month")
2. **You issue invoice** to broker:
   - Invoice to: XTB (their company details)
   - Service: "Affiliate marketing services - {month}"
   - Amount: ‚Ç¨500 (or RON equivalent)
   - VAT: Add 19% if you're VAT registered, exempt if micro-company
3. Send invoice to broker
4. Receive payment to SRL bank account
5. Declare in quarterly reports

**VAT Considerations**:
- **Micro-company (<‚Ç¨25,000 revenue)**: VAT exempt
- **Standard SRL (>‚Ç¨25,000)**: Must charge 19% VAT
- **Reverse charge**: If broker is in EU, may use reverse charge (no VAT, they handle it)

**Documents Needed**:
- Commission reports from broker
- Invoices you issued
- Bank transfer confirmations
- Contract/agreement with broker

### Tax Obligations for SRL

#### Micro-Company Regime (Recommended for Year 1-2)

**Eligibility**:
- Revenue < ‚Ç¨500,000/year
- Not in restricted activities (banking, insurance, gambling)
- At least 1 employee or shareholder-manager

**Tax Rate**:
- **1%** on revenue if you have employees
- **3%** on revenue if no employees (just shareholder-manager)

**Example** (Year 1: ‚Ç¨2,000 revenue):
- Tax: ‚Ç¨2,000 √ó 3% = ‚Ç¨60
- Declare quarterly, pay quarterly
- Very simple accounting

**Dividend Distribution**:
- After paying corporate tax, remaining profit can be distributed as dividends
- Dividend tax: 8% (paid by shareholder, not SRL)
- Example: ‚Ç¨2,000 revenue ‚Üí ‚Ç¨60 tax ‚Üí ‚Ç¨1,940 profit ‚Üí distribute ‚Ç¨1,940 ‚Üí pay 8% (‚Ç¨155) ‚Üí net ‚Ç¨1,785

#### Standard SRL Regime (If Revenue > ‚Ç¨500k)

**Tax Rate**:
- **16%** corporate income tax on profit (revenue - expenses)
- More complex accounting, can deduct expenses
